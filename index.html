1.0 introduction

1.1 overview

lectric cars operate by drawing energy from the battery to power the motor when the vehicle is turned on. The controller regulates the power delivered to the motor based on driver input, ensuring smooth acceleration and cruising. Regenerative braking captures kinetic energy to recharge the battery during deceleration. Charging the vehicle involves plugging it into an external power source, such as a home charger or public charging station. The advantages of electric cars include zero tailpipe emissionszero tailpipe emissions, making them environmentally friendly, higher energy efficiency compared to internal combustion engines, and lower operating costs due to fewer moving parts and the lower price of electricity. They also provide a quiet, smooth driving experience with instant torque. However, challenges remain, including limited driving range, the high initial cost of the vehicle, insufficient charging infrastructure in some areas, and concerns about battery life and recycling. Electric vehicles are evolving rapidly, with advancements in batteryadvancements in battery technology, such as solid-state batteries and faster charging systems, improving their performance and accessibility. The integration of autonomous driving technology and innovations like vehicle-to-grid (V2G) energy sharing further highlight the future potential of EVs. As they become more affordable and widespread, electric cars are set to play a significant role in reducing greenhouse gas emissions and promoting sustainable transportation.

1.2 scope

The project scope include:

  1. Objective and Goals:
    • Develop a fully functional prototype of an electric car.
    • Achieve specific performance metrics, such as driving range, speed, and energy efficiency.
  2. Key Components:
    • Design and integration of the battery pack and energy storage system.
    • Selection and implementation of an electric motor and inverter system.
    • Development of a regenerative braking system.
  3. Software and Control Systems:
    • Development of the power control unit (PCU) for managing energy distribution.
    • Design and implementation of a user interface for real-time monitoring (e.g., battery status, speed).
